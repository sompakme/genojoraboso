Justin Bieber Will Make Thousands For Upcoming 'Friends' Reunion Cameo

Publish date: 2024-08-22

Justin Bieber could rake in thousands for his upcoming cameo in Friends. The highly-anticipated reunion special is set to air this year on HBO Max.

According to The Sun, Bieber will wear the “Spudnik” costume that David Schwimmer’s character, Ross wore during the Season 8 finale of Friends.

A source said that Bieber is thrilled to be part of Friends even though he would have to wear the embarrassing potato costume.

“Justin was honored to be involved and didn’t mind sending himself up wearing a ridiculous costume. It was all run like a military operation to keep details under wraps, and Justin nailed it,” the source said.

This isn’t Bieber’s only cameo. The “Holy” singer previously made a cameo in Zoolander, where he portrayed himself in the 2016 hit movie. In 2014, Bieber also appeared in Behaving Badly, which starred his ex-girlfriend, Selena Gomez.

The “As I Am” singer also appeared in Popstar: Never Stop Never Stopping alongside other musicians like Akon, DJ Khaled, and more.

Other than his appearances in movies, Bieber has also made cameos in music videos. He recently appeared in the music video for “Popstar” alongside his wife, Hailey Baldwin.

It is unclear how much Bieber makes for his cameos, but they are certainly a part of the singer’s massive net worth. However, a huge chunk of his salary comes from his music.

Still, Looper revealed that Brad Pitt received $1,000 for appearing in Deadpool 2. And Alec Baldwin reportedly earned $250,000 after appearing in Glengarry Glen Ross. The actor was on set for just three days.

Meanwhile, Celebrity Net Worth revealed that Bieber’s salary is estimated at $80 million. And his net worth is $285 million.

During his touring years, Bieber was known as one of the highest-paid entertainers in the world.

In 2007, famed music manager Scooter Braun accidentally clicked on one of Bieber’s videos on YouTube, and he was impressed with what he heard.

The producer immediately tracked Bieber down and urged the singer’s mom to bring him to Atlanta so that he could record some demo tapes. A week later, Bieber sang for Usher, who happens to be Braun’s partner at Raymond Braun Media Group (RBMG).

Three years later, Bieber dropped his two-part album, My World and My World 2.0, which were a massive success. His third album, Believe also sold 3 million copies across the globe.

Despite his success, things haven’t always been easy for Bieber. In the documentary Seasons, Bieber revealed his struggles with drugs and alcohol.

Today, Bieber seems to be in a much better place in his life. He dropped new songs, and he has also been spending a lot of time with his wife in their $26 million mansion in Beverly Hills.
