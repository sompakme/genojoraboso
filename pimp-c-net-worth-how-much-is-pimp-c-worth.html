Pimp C Biography

Born on December 29, 1973, in Port Arthur, Texas, Pimp C (Chad Lamont Butler) was a prominent rap artist known for his contributions to the southern hip hop scene. He formed the rap duo Underground Kingz (UGK) with his close friend Bun B, and together, they created a unique sound that blended the rawness of street life with catchy melodies.

Pimp C’s career as a rap artist was filled with both triumphs and challenges. In 2002, he faced an eight-year prison sentence, which interrupted his flourishing career. However, despite the setback, Pimp C remained dedicated to his craft, continuing to work on his music and releasing his solo album, “Pimpalation,” in 2006.

Tragically, Pimp C’s life was cut short when he was found dead in his hotel room on December 4, 2007. The cause of death was determined to be a drug overdose, highlighting the darker side of the music industry and the struggles that many artists face.

Despite the untimely end to his life, Pimp C’s legacy continues to resonate in the rap community. His lyrical genius, unique style, and contributions to the southern hip hop genre have influenced aspiring artists and shaped the landscape of the music industry.
