Big Time Rush And One Direction

Big Time Rush and One Direction were tourmates in 2012. One Direction opened the tour for the pop band as an in-house record label mates.

Kendall unveiled that they weren’t happy with the fact that One Direction was opening for them. He stated that there were 2000 girls outside their show jamming to their songs although they were headlining the tour.

The founder of the band, Schmidt, also stated that they felt like being used by their record label to promote One Direction.  He also added that the only prime reason the two boy bands were together on a tour was that they were from the same record label.

Is Big Time Rush A Real Band?

Yes, Big Time Rush is a real band. Four of them were brought together through auditions by producers like many other bands.

Ever since the creation of their band, all four of them have been working hard on forging their names in the music scene even after more than a decade after its inception.

The boy band was signed to Nick Records in 2009 and later transferred to Columbia Records. As a group, all four boys starred in the TV series, Big Time Rush, which ran from November 28, 2009, to July 25, 2013.

For the series, the band released their eponymous debut single on November 29, 2009. The show also saw the releases of the band’s promotional singles including ‘Any Kind of Guy’ and ‘City is Ours.’
