Mike Trout is an American professional baseball player with an estimated net worth of $10 million.

Mike Trout started playing baseball with the Ripken League and the Little League. He earned five letters and played baseball and basketball for Millville Senior High School. He was a pitcher and shortstop before shifting to the outfield during his senior year with the team.

He committed to play for the East Carolina University baseball team. Trout played travel ball for the Tri-State Arsenal. He has also played in several tournaments with the Arsenal. He was selected 25th overall by the Los Angeles Angels of Anaheim during the 2009 MLB Draft.

Trout played for the Angels' minor league affiliate, Arizona League's Arizona Angels. During his stint with the team, he made a .360 hit with a .418 OBP and .506 SLG, and 1 home run. Other teams that he played for include Class A Midwest League's Cedar Rapids Kernels, Class A-Advanced California League's Rancho Cucamonga Quakes, and Class AA Texas League's Arkansas Travelers.

In 2011, he made his debut with the Angels. Trout appeared in four All-Star games from 2012 to 2015 and twice won All-Star MVP . He was a three-time Silver Slugger awardee and won the Hank Aaron in 2014. On April 17, 2015, he was hailed as the youngest player in MLB history to reach the 100 home runs and 100 stolen bases.

Michael Nelson Trout was born on August 7, 1991 in Millville, New Jersey.
