Twelve years in and 23 movies later, the Marvel Cinematic Universe has accumulated total earnings of $8.545 billion domestically and $22.588 billion worldwide. Based on this number, the MCU is considered to be the most lucrative movie franchise of all time. When Spider-Man: No Way Home was released in cinemas, it has broken numerous records and even climbed the ladder as the third highest grossing movie in the Marvel Cinematic Universe franchise. Considering that the movie was released during the pandemic which may have hindered huge amount of people from going to the movie theaters, this is a huge win for the MCU. Many may be wondering, which Marvel Cinematic Universe movie has made to the top ten highest grossing movies, the top movies are listed below.

Captain Marvel

Captain Marvel comes in at the tenth spot with a total of $426.8 million domestic gross and $1.128 billion worldwide box office. The movie released last March 8, 2019 had just about $160 to $175 million budget range for the movie, this is great return on investment for the distributor of the movie Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The movie directed by Anna Boden and Ryan Fleck is about the extraterrestrial Kree warrior, Captain Marvel, who found herself caught in the middle of an intergalactic feud between the Skrulls and her people. It is up to her to end the war using her special superpowers.

Spiderman: Far From Home

Spiderman: Far From Home comes in at the ninth spot with a total of $390.5 million domestic gross and $1.13 billion worldwide box office. The movie released last July 2, 2019 had just about $160 million budget range for the movie; this is great return on investment for the distributor of the movie Sony Pictures Entertainment. The movie directed by Jon Watts is about the relaxing European trip of Peter Parker which soon got interrupted when Nick Fury showed up at his hotel room to recruit him for a mission. With world being endangered by as four massive elemental creatures -- each representing Earth, water, air and fire, it is up to Spiderman to save the day with the help of Fury and fellow superhero Mysterio.

Captain America: Civil War

Captain America: Civil War comes in at the eighth spot with a total of $408 million domestic gross and $1.15 billion worldwide box office. The movie released last May 6, 2016 had just about $250 million budget range for the movie, this is great return on investment for the distributor of the movie Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The movie directed by Joe Russo and Anthony Russo is about the political pressure to monitor the movements of the Avengers lead which then lead to a collateral damage. This movement has divided the members of Avengers, one team lead by Captain America who believes that they should still be free to defend the humanity without the interference of the government and Iron Man who thinks that they need to follow the government on this one.

Iron Man 3

Iron Man 3 comes in at the seventh spot with a total of $409 million domestic gross and $1.2 billion worldwide box office. The movie released last May 3, 2013 had just about $200 million budget range for the movie, this is great return on investment for the distributor of the movie Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The movie directed by Shane Black is about Tony Stark plagued by worry and insomnia after he saved New York from destruction. This has affected all the aspect of his life which includes his relationship with Pepper.

Avengers: Age of Ultron

Avengers: Age of Ultron comes in at the fifth spot with a total of $459 million domestic gross and $1.4 billion worldwide box office. The movie released last May 01, 2015 had just about $250 million budget range for the movie, this is great return on investment for the distributor of the movie Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. The movie directed by Joss Whedon is about Tony Stark who jumpstarts a dormant peacekeeping program which went terribly and forced the Avengers including Thor and Incredible Hulk to reassemble.

The Avengers

The Avengers comes in at the fourth spot with a total of $220 million domestic gross and $1.5 billion worldwide box office. The movie released last May 4, 2012 had just about $220 million budget range for the movie, this is great return on investment for the distributor of the movie Walt Disney Studios Motion Pictures. This is where the dream team was formed which consists of Hawkeye (Jeremy Renner), the Black Widow (Scarlett Johansson), Thor (Chris Hemsworth), the Hulk (Mark Ruffalo), Captain America (Chris Evans), and Iron Man (Robert Downey Jr.).
