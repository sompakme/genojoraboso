Oprah Winfrey School

Publish date: 2024-08-29

The Oprah Winfrey Leadership Academy For Girls in Henley-on-Klip, South Africa comprises 152 girls especially hand-picked by Oprah from 3500 applicants. These lucky girls can text, call or email their "Mom-Oprah" whenever they needed help or advice.

Few shots of the classrooms at the Oprah Winfrey Leadership Academy for Girls  were classes are held.

The girls in their green uniform, Oprah's favorite color. These bright uniforms were chosen by Oprah herself has an 'O' embroidered on the pocket of each blazer.

The Foyer which resembles a work of art. This serves as an art gallery displaying few of the works of 500 African artists.

The School's Choir is a great aspect of how the girls like to pass their time. These girls sing a gospel song in English and Zulu every Monday morning  assembly ending it on a high note.

The Graduation Day! Oprah and her 72 graduates with a victorious smiles on their faces. These girls are now enjoying university life. Seven of them are in Wellesly and Spelman  colleges in the U.S.
